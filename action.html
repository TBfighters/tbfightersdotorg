Take Action

Tuberculosis is a complex social justice issue, so we tackle it from multiple angles. This includes government policy, funding, and access to testing and treatments. You can join us and help save lives by demanding affordable testing, advocating to protect government global health funding, or educating yourself and others. Pick the lanes that look most interesting, fun, or important to you!

Things change quickly in the TB landscape! To stay updated on the latest calls to action, follow TBFighters on social media and subscribe to our newsletter.

    • Affordable Testing

    Danaher, a large biotechnology company, makes some of the best-in-class TB tests, yet they grossly overprice the tests for the people who can least afford it. In 2023, after pressure from TBFighters and others, Danaher promised to sell their basic GeneXpert tests at cost. Since then, we have seen no proof that their tests truly are being sold at cost. TBFighters are keeping up an active campaign to remind Danaher of their promises and urge them to lower the prices on all of their tests, such as drug-resistant TB, HIV, hepatitis, and many sexually transmitted infections.

    US Hill Day

    TBFighters and partners travel to Washington DC each year to advocate to Congressional offices for increased funding for tuberculosis prevention, testing, and treatment. This is a fantastic opportunity to learn directly from TB experts and survivors, participate in extremely effective advocacy, and have fun!

    Tell Canada to #LeadOnTB

    Many in wealthy countries think tuberculosis is history, but the reality is stark: over 80% of TB cases and deaths occur in low- and middle-income countries. And here in Canada, Inuit in Nunavut experience TB rates more than 300 times higher than the non-Indigenous population. TB is not just a global issue-it's a Canadian one too.

    Global Fund

    The Global Fund is the world's largest funder of global health grants, fighting tuberculosis, HIV/AIDS, and Malaria. Its continued funding is crucial to the fight against TB around the world. This year nations are considering how much funding they should give to the Global Fund as a part of its 2026-2028 replenishment cycle. We need to remind our governments to keep their commitments to global health and replenish the Global Fund!
