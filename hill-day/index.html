What is Hill Day?

TB Hill Day is an annual event where TBFighters, along with our partners in TB advocacy (including PIH, the TB Roundtable, We Are TB, and more), advocate for more TB funding directly to our representatives on Capitol Hill.

On March 2, we will attend a training day run by our partner organizations to prepare everyone for our meetings and give us time to meet with our groups.

On March 3, groups of advocates meet with senators and representatives (or their staff) in Washington, DC and ask them directly to increase TB funding domestically and globally.

Funding Defended

Budget proposals released after Hill Day 2025 made minimal cuts to tuberculosis related funding, even though the White House had attempted to cut TB funding by half.

Increased Awareness

Hill Day increases the education and awareness of people in congress about TB. During our meetings, we inform, often unaware congresspeople and staffers, of the problem many are facing due to TB. In 2025 alone, we informed over 200 offices of the problem, helping to ensure they will keep TB in mind while voting.

Community Strengthened

Hill Day brings together TB advocates, experts, and survivors from around the US to not only lobby their congressional offices but to meet each other. In 2025, over 200 survivors, advocates, and experts convened in DC, providing valuable opportunities for connection and community building.
