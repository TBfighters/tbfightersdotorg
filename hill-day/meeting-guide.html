Meet With Your Representatives - US


TB Hill Day is not just an in-person event! If you couldn’t make it this year, we encourage you to join in by:

  1. Asking your elected official to sign on to a TB Dear Colleague Letter or thanking them if they have.
  2. Scheduling a virtual/local meeting with your member of Congress if we did not get the chance to meet with them on the Hill!

If you’re a member of any of the districts listed in this spreadsheet, join in by scheduling a meeting! If you live in South Dakota or Arkansas we’d love you to schedule a meeting with your Senators as well.

If you don’t see your district there, view the RESULTS Dear Colleague Letter Scorecard to see whether to call on your member to sign the letter or thank them for already signing, and go to our templates page for more information!

Scheduling Your Meeting

All members of Congress maintain offices in their state/district. You can ask to meet at one of their local offices or meet virtually, whichever works for you! To schedule a meeting, email the office with your request. You can find contact information using Congress’ Find Your Members tool. An example email can be found below. Send the email through the contact form on your member’s site or an email address there. Also keep an eye out for a scheduling form on their site! In your email, you should make sure to note whether you’d like the meeting to take place online or in a local office.

Example Scheduling Email

Subject: Constituent meeting request on domestic and global TB programs


Hi, I’m a constituent from [your town]. I'd like to meet [virtually, online | in the member’s local office at address] for 20-30 minutes to discuss domestic and global TB programs with the member or staff if the member is unavailable. Would the member have any time on [dates or times when you’re available] to speak with me?


Thank you,

[name]

Schedule this email to send at 9 AM ET so it's at the top of the staffer’s inbox—emails can get lost! We also suggest following up every couple of days by email or putting in a call to the member’s local office until you have the meeting scheduled.

Email us at tbhillday@gmail.com to let us know if you’d like a fellow advocate to join your meeting, and we’ll do our best to match you up with someone!

The Meeting

To prepare for your meeting, go over statistics, talking points (linked below), and plan what you’re going to say! Meeting should follow a general outline of:

  1. Introductions.
  2. Why you care/how TB affects you and your community.
  3. Global asks
  4. Domestic asks
  5. Thank you

But be flexible! Your goal is to maintain a conversation with the member or their staff, not lecture them. Take notes on your conversation, and don’t be scared to say “I don’t know” and follow up after your meeting!


You can watch our webinar with Partners In Health on scheduling meetings from the 30-minute mark for a mock meeting. You can also find some talking points. Don’t worry too much about following the script perfectly—just sharing the top line asks and why you’re passionate about TB are the most important parts. It is our representatives’ and their staff’s job to meet with constituents and hear where they want government resources allocated.


For information on TB in your area and your member’s record on TB legislation, see the Congressional Tuberculosis Tracker by TBFighter Tofu. For our asks to the member and their staffer, consult the talking points page.

Afterwards

Submit our form to let us know what you’ve accomplished! Within a few days of your meeting, send an email to the staffer you met with or who facilitated your meeting. This is your opportunity to thank them for their time, elaborate on your discussion, and answer any questions! Below are templates for House and Senate follow-ups—email us at tbhillday@gmail.com if you need any help answering questions.
