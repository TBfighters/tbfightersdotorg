Meet With Your Representatives - US

Note: this guide is not a guide for the in-person hill day event.

Scheduling Your Meeting

Local Meetings

All members of Congress maintain offices in their state/district. To schedule a meeting, we suggest you either call their local office or email them, you can find both at congress' Find Your Member page. An example email can be found below. In your email, you should make sure to note that your meeting will take place in the district office.

Find you member of congress

Example Scheduling Email

Subject: Constituent meeting request on domestic and global TB programs


Hi I’m a constituent from (your town). I'd like to meet in person at your local office for 20-30 minutes to discuss domestic and global TB programs with the member or staff if the member is unavailable. Would the member have any time on that day to speak with us?


Thank you,

(name)

We suggest sending this email in the morning so its at the top of the Representatives' inbox (they get a lot a day). We also suggest following up every couple of days if you haven't gotten a response.

The Meeting

You will most likely be meeting with a member of the staff, not the Representative or Senator themselves. That’s totally okay. The staffer will be nothing but respectful towards you, even if they are a rupublican. For the meeting itself, we have made a guide for you to follow! You can find it below along with a button to print it out.


Although even meeting will look slightly different, you should aim to have a conversation with the staffer/representative, rather than just lecturing them about TB. You should always start off with introductions, inform them about tuberculosis and tell them the asks (but it won't always happen in that order). But this largely depends on the staffer/representative themselves. Oftentimes, the staffer/representative will ask you questions, if you know the answer: feel free the answer, but if you do not saying "I don't know" will not hurt you case, but you should follow up later with the answer! You also don't have to memorize every statistic, word, or talking point, you should have gone over the points (and possibly practiced a few time), but you can always refer back to the talking points at anytime and it will not hurt your case!


As the meeting wraps up, you should leave behind a few papers that give an overview of our asks and the general talking points. We have provided you with PDFs below that you can print out. If this is not an option for you, or you are meeting virtually, you should send the PDFs afterwards in an email!

Afterwards

Afterwards, make sure to follow up with them, especially if they had any questions. In the follow up email, you should include the asks and a quick overview of our talking points. We have provided a email below for both the house an senate to make it easier for you to send said follow up email.

Example House Follow-up Email

Hi (Staffer Name),


Thank you for taking the time to meet with me [add other names if you’d like, or just say “other TB Fighters” etc.] on April 9th to discuss the importance of FY26 appropriations for global and domestic tuberculosis priorities. Below is our two-page summary.


[Feel free to highlight something specific you spoke about here, answer questions they had, or just reiterate your thanks.]


We hope your office will sign onto our FY26 Dear Colleague letter led by Rep. Wagner and Rep. Bera. Please reach out with any questions!


Thanks for all you do!!


Best,

(name)

Remember to include the two page PDF of our asks attached to the email.
