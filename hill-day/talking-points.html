TB Facts

You do not have to memorize any of these.


Broad

  • Most TB is fully curable (and has been for over 50 years).
  • TB adversely affects communities with greater obstacles to health.
    • Drivers of TB globally include poverty-related risk factors such as malnutrition, crowded and poorly ventilated living and working conditions.


Domestic

  • In 2024, there were over 10,000 cases in the US and over 500 deaths.
  • The US saw an 8% increase in TB cases in 2024.
  • It mostly affects marginalized communities.

Why its important

Here are some reasons to give to your representative on why funding domestic TB efforts is important (but please customize these or throw in new ones when you meet with your representative!):

  • Racial Justice:
    • 88.1% of reported TB cases in the US are among racial and ethnic minority groups according to the CDC.
    • People who spend time in crowded living conditions with poor access to medical care, such as homeless shelters and correctional facilities also have higher rates of TB.
  • Cost-savings:
    • There are huge cost-saving benefits to investing in this preventing and mitigating the effects of an outbreak.
    • Over 20 years, U.S. TB control efforts averted up to $14.5 billion in medical and societal costs from TB deaths.
  • Local Impacts:
    • You can find state level information at the CDC's TB report in 2023. These statistics are really useful for customizing your talking points to you state to bring home the message.


International

  • TB kills 1.25 million people each year.
  • 10.8 million people developed TB in 2023.
    • 1.3 million children developed TB in 2023.
  • TB represents 30% of deaths from antimicrobial resistant bacteria.

Why it's important

Here are some reasons to give to your representative on why funding international TB efforts is important (but please customize these or throw in new ones when you meet with your representative!):

  • Maternal and Child Health:
    • Every day, almost 600 children die from TB.
    • TB accounts for 6–15% of all maternal mortality.
  • Cost-savings:
    • This investment will save us billions of dollars in the long run.
    • For example, it was more expensive upfront to eradicate smallpox, but the United States saves all of its spending on smallpox every 26 days because it does not have to vaccinate or treat the disease anymore.
  • Diplomacy:
    • In 2023, 110 retired three and four-star generals urged Congress to support robust funding for global development projects, including USAID, as they wrote that these diplomatic programs “are critical to preventing conflict and reducing the need to put our men and women in uniform in harm’s way.”
    • A healthier, more stable world is better for everyone and funding for tuberculosis builds goodwill for the United States

The Programs

Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)

The CDC has two divisions of interest: the Division of TB Elimination and the Division of Global HIV and TB, both of which still exist. These divisions are paramount for treating tuberculosis patients in the US.


United States Agency for International Development (USAID)

Although USAID has been dismantled, it has helped millions of people around the world get the drugs and testing they need to survive.


The Global Fund

The Global Fund creates a lot of the groundwork for distributing drugs to the people who need them. But, like many other agencies, recent funding cuts have slowed its work, making it harder (if not impossible) for people to continue their treatment


National Institute of Health (NIH)

The NIH is very important to the continued research and development of everything related to TB. But due to recent funding cuts, TB research—which has already been underfunded—is decimated.


Congressionally Directed Medical Research Programs (CDMRP) - Senate Only

The CDMRP is a research branch of the Department of Defense that researches diseases internationally.

Our Asks

Domestic

CDC

Protect CDC’s Division of TB Elimination; sustain spending at congressionally directed levels

  • Support $225 million in FY 2026 for the CDC’s Division of TB Elimination
  • Support $21 million in FY 2026 for the CDC’s Division of Global HIV and TB

NIH

Support at least $51.303 billion for NIH (as a whole)

  • Support at least $7.29 billion for the National Institute of Allergy and Infectious Diseases (NIAID).
  • Protect the structure of NIAID and its support for TB research.


International

USAID

Restore staff and increase funding for global health TB programs

  • Support $1 billion in Fiscal Year 2026 for global bilateral TB efforts.
  • Require additional and more frequent reporting that demonstrates appropriated funds are being spent as intended.

The Global Fund

  • Support a contribution of $2 billion to the Global Fund to Fight AIDS, TB and Malaria. This would unlock another $4 billion other donors during this replenishment year.

CDMRP - Senate only

Include TB as an eligible disease in the Congressionally Directed Medical Research Program’s Peer Reviewed Medical Research Program & restore full funding to the program.
