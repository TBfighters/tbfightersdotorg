Danaher, it's time for $5

Tuberculosis is curable, yet 1.6 million people die of tuberculosis every year while Danaher price gouges diagnostic tests for the patients who can least afford it.

(image)

Tuberculosis (TB) is the world's most deadly infectious disease.

A total of 1.6 million people died from tuberculosis in 2021–with more than 30% being undiagnosed.

Worldwide, TB is the 15th leading cause of death and the second leading infectious killer after COVID-19 (above HIV and AIDS).

TB symptoms may be mild for many months, so it is easy to spread TB to others without knowing it.

...and it's curable

An estimated 4 million lives were saved from TB diagnosis and treatment between 2000 and 2021.

However, access to TB treatment and diagnosis is highly unequal globally. Only about 1 in 3 people with drug resistant TB accessed treatment in 2021.

(image)

Enter Danaher.

Danaher is a large and powerful corporation based in the U.S. that owns multiple biotech companies, including Cepheid. Cepheid is a molecular diagnostics company that–through their GeneXpert diagnostics technology–has revolutionized diagnosis of many diseases, including tuberculosis.

With just one test, doctors can quickly determine not only if a patient has TB, but if it is resistant to certain type of medicines, making treatment more effective.

Sounds great, right?

Profits over People.

However, Danaher uses what many call and "printer ink" business model when it comes to GeneXpert. If you've ever bought printer ink, you know that the printer is usually sold for a reasonable price, but the ink is often sold at a very inflated price. Danaher prices GeneXpert in a similar way.

The testing machines are moderately priced, but a single test cartridge can run upwards of $10-15. And for low income and impoverished communities that need it the most, that's simply not affordable.
