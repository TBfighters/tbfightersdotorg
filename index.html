Danaher, it's Time for $5

Tuberculosis is curable, yet 1.6 million people die of tuberculosis every year while Danaher overcharges for diagnostic tests for the patients who can least afford it.

Tuberculosis (TB) is the world's deadliest infectious disease.

A total of 1.6 million people died from tuberculosis in 2021–with more than 30% being undiagnosed.

Worldwide, TB is the 13th leading cause of death and the leading infectious killer after COVID-19 (above HIV and AIDS).
TB symptoms may be mild for many months, so it is easy to spread TB to others without knowing it.

...and it's curable

An estimated 74 million lives were saved from TB diagnosis and treatment between 2000 and 2021. However, TB treatment and diagnosis have unfairly high prices to the point that many who need it can't afford it.
Only about 1 in 3 people with drug-resistant TB accessed treatment in 2021.

Enter Danaher.

Danaher - a large U.S. corporation - owns many smaller biotech companies, including Cepheid.
Cepheid designs and manufactures diagnostic tests. Their TB GeneXpert test determines whether a patient has TB and if it is resistant to certain TB drugs, enabling effective treatment.
GeneXpert tests give fast results without a need for a fully equipped laboratory. That should make them an accessible option everywhere.

Sounds great, right?

Profits over People.

However, Danaher uses what many call a “printer ink” business model when it comes to GeneXpert. While a printer is usually sold for a reasonable price, the ink is often extremely expensive, with a high profit margin. Danaher prices GeneXpert in a similar way.
The reusable machines used to process these tests are moderately priced, but an individual test cartridge can run upwards of $10-15 dollars.

In Sierra Leone - a country with a very high rate of tuberculosis - that is the same as the average wage for an entire week. That's simply not affordable.

We are calling on Cepheid and Danaher to prioritize people's lives over maximizing profits by dropping the price of all GeneXpert tests, which cost about $3 to make, to $5.
