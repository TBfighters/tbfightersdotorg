TUBERCULOSIS IS THE WORLDS DEADLIEST INFECTIOUS DISEASE.

You might be lucky enough to live in a place where people don’t think about TB. But in 2023, 10 million people were diagnosed with TB, and 1.25 million people were killed by it.


We’ve been able to treat TB for a hundred years. Why do we still let millions die? Testing and treatment are prohibitively expensive, and access to appropriate care is inequitably distributed. One in four people with TB will never be diagnosed, and one in three will never get treatment. It’s not a medical mystery—it’s a human one.

WE ARE TBFIGHTERS

We are a community of Nerdfighters and global health activists committed to fighting the structural causes of TB.


We partner with organizations like Partners in Health and Médecins Sans Frontières to bring attention and resources to this disease of inequality. We listen deeply to global health professionals, choose our advocacy targets accordingly, and bring commitment and community (and fun!) to the cause. Using this model, we are able to effect change in both private and public spaces. We believe in leading by following.
